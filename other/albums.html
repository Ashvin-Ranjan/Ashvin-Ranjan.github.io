Albums You Might Not Know
How Are We Feeling Today?
Artist: Kaizo Slumber
Recommendations: LONG LIVE SLUMBER, GALAXY TORIMOTI, SUDOKU
Death Grips if they were extremely online is how I would describe Kaizo Slumber, not in a bad way. The sound of this album is loud, and the screaming vocals are extremely powerful when combined with the hard electric guitar, insane drum loops, and heavy use of sampling. Overall, an extremely creative album, transitioning from rock, to calm jazz, to Vietnam War-era protest song all in 25 minutes. The album also has one of my favorite covers to boot.
Generation Y
Artist: Devon Hendryx
Recommendations: Monáde, Lightbulbs
Generation Y is the first mixtape of Devon Hendryx, later renaming as JPEGMAFIA. The album mixes amazing beats, tight flows, and very meaningful messages into a very enjoyable listening experience from beginning to end, with some of my favorite songs JPEGMAFIA has produced on the mixtape. However, it has been taken off of Bandcamp and Spotify for uncleared samples, so you can only find it unoffically reuploaded on YouTube. Despite this, it is definitely worth a listen.
Brazil By Ear 3
Artist: your best friend jippy
Recommendations: International Travel, The Current, Suitcase Full of Records
Brazil By Ear 3 is an amazing listening experience by your best friend jippy, an amazing producer. The album contains many amazing and memorable songs, and one of my favorite songs of all time "The Current." I highly recommand you give it a listen.
Brazil By Ear 2
Artist: your best friend jippy
Recommendations: Acarajé, Fernando de Noronha
Brazil By Ear 2 is in similar taste to Brazil By Ear 3, and while it is shorter the average quality of the songs stays at a higher bar, making for a very nice album. While the highs of this album do not match the highs of Brazil By Ear 3, the general tone and quality of the entire album is consistently enjoyable and is definitely worth a listen.
Rikki
Artist: Ginger Root
Recommendations: Out of State, Phone a Friend, Rikki
Admittedly (and rightfully so) one of the more popular albums on this list, Rikki is one of very few no-skip albums I have ever encountered. Every single song in Rikki holds its own and it has some fan-favorites such as "Karaoke" and "Le château." While popular among fans of Ginger Root, I still feel as though the album is criminally underrated, as it holds some of Ginger Root's best work.
